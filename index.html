About Me

I am Murali Ganesh Peddireddy, a dynamic and results-oriented chemical engineering professional with a robust academic background and hands-on experience in the petroleum refining industry. I hold an MS in Chemical Engineering from the State University of New York, Buffalo, and a B.Tech in Chemical Engineering from SRM Institute of Science and Technology, India.

During my tenure at Nayara Energy Limited in Jamnagar, India, I started as a Graduate Engineer Trainee and was later promoted to Assistant Manager, where I oversaw operations in the Vacuum Gas Oil Unit, including the Delayed Coker Unit. My responsibilities included managing operations during refinery turnarounds and handling emergency situations, which earned me recognition for a seamless start-up of critical equipment. I have also gained valuable experience as an Industrial Trainee at Technior India Engineering and Mayura Industries, where I contributed to pipeline infrastructure projects and gained hands-on experience in the production of palm oil and biodiesel.

My academic and professional journey has equipped me with proficiency in simulation tools such as Unisim and Aspen, along with programming languages like Matlab, C, and Python. I am committed to continuous learning and innovation, as evidenced by my coursework and participation in various certificate programs, including Six Sigma and Quantum Mechanics. Beyond my professional endeavors, I am passionate about organizing technical events and contributing to the community. I have served as the Head of the Technical domain in 'The SRM Chemical Club' and as the Graphic Design Coordinator for the Chemflux 8.0 Conference, where I designed promotional materials and visual content.

With a blend of technical expertise, practical experience, and a passion for innovation, I am eager to contribute to challenging chemical engineering projects and make a meaningful impact in the field.
